Design Analysis & Reflection

While making this portfolio website, my main goal was to build something that shows off my design work in a clean, organized way. Since I want to focus on branding and layout, I knew the website itself needed to look good and follow basic design rules. By using proximity, alignment, repetition, contrast, and typography, I was able to turn basic HTML code into a professional-looking site.

Proximity

Definition: Proximity just means grouping related things together so people know they belong to the same idea. Things that are close together look connected, and things spaced apart look different.

Implementation: I used proximity a lot in my CSS spacing. For example, in my header, I used flexbox to put my logo right next to my name so people immediately know whose site it is. In my portfolio gallery, I made sure the captions sit right under the images they belong to, so users don't get confused about which text goes with which project.

Alignment

Definition: Alignment is how text or images line up with each other on the page (like left, right, or center). It helps keep the page looking organized and not messy.

Implementation: I kept my main content centered on the page using a max-width and auto margins, so it doesn't stretch out weirdly on big screens. I also made sure all my normal paragraphs were aligned to the left, which is just easier for people to read than center-aligned text.

Repetition

Definition: Repetition is using the same colors, fonts, or layouts over and over throughout a project. It ties everything together and makes it feel consistent.

Implementation: Repetition is what makes this site feel like one big project instead of a bunch of random pages. I used the exact same header, footer, and navigation bar everywhere. I also used the same orange-red color across the site. It shows up in the header, the active links, and the buttons, so the user gets used to seeing it.

Contrast

Definition: Contrast is putting two very different things together—like dark and light or big and small—to make the important stuff stand out and grab your attention.

Implementation: I mainly used color contrast. Most of the website has dark text on a light background so it is easy to read. But to make the navigation bar and footer pop, I gave them a really dark background with white text. I also added a drop-shadow when you hover over the gallery images to make them stand out from the flat background.

Typography

Definition: Typography is picking the right fonts and arranging the text so it looks good and is easy to read on the screen.

Implementation: Since I love typography, picking the fonts was my favorite part. I used 'Poppins' for my big headers because it looks clean and modern, and 'IBM Plex Mono' for the normal text because it looks kind of technical like code. I also made sure the lines of text had enough space between them so people wouldn't get tired reading it.

In the end, hand-coding this site helped me see how regular graphic design rules also apply to building websites. By keeping these five ideas in mind, I made a site that isn't just a folder for my homework, but a good piece of design on its own.
